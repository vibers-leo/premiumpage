Key technologies for future mobility control
  • AGV / AMR Navigation
    AGV/AMR control technology utilizes sensor fusion–based SLAM for real-time localization and enables safe and efficient movement through path planning and obstacle avoidance algorithms. It also improves operational efficiency through low-power motor control and battery optimization, while enabling integrated management of multiple robots via API-linked fleet management systems
  • Physical AI
    This technology combines AI algorithms with physical systems, enabling robots and machinery to perceive, judge, and act autonomously in real-world environments. By integrating various sensors such as Vision, LiDAR, tactile, and force sensors, it understands the surroundings and object states in real time to generate context-aware actions
  • Personal Mobility / Assistive Tech
    By leveraging sensor fusion–based perception technologies, the system recognizes the user’s state and surrounding environment in real time. Using vision, ultrasonic sensors, IMU, and biometric sensors, it detects obstacles, ground conditions, and user movement. Based on this information, optimized services are provided by applying navigation guidance and collision avoidance algorithms, enabling safe and stable mobility in both indoor and outdoor environments.
  • Drone Delivery Control
    Through advanced flight control algorithms and optimized thrust and attitude control, the system enables stable hovering and precise takeoff and landing performance. By leveraging a battery management system (BMS) and energy-efficient flight strategies, it maximizes flight time and transportation efficiency. In addition, a communication-integrated control and fleet management system enables centralized operation of multiple drones, supporting mission assignment, real-time flight status monitoring, and rapid response to abnormal situations—thereby delivering safe and efficient drone delivery services.
  • Maritime Autonomy Systems
    The maritime autonomous navigation system recognizes the ship's position, attitude, and surrounding marine environment in real time by utilizing sensor fusion-based navigation technologies (GNSS, IMU, Radar, LiDAR, AIS, etc.). Through this, stable route tracking is possible even under complex maritime conditions such as weather changes, waves, and currents, and it supports safe navigation that complies with the International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea (COLREGs) by applying autonomous route planning and collision avoidance algorithms.
